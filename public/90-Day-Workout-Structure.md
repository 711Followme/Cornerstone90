# FORGE: 90-Day Workout Structure
## Complete Progressive Training Plan

### Theme: Building Strength - Body, Mind, and Spirit

---

## Overview

**Duration**: 90 Days (13 Weeks)
**Structure**: 6 days training, 1 day Sabbath rest
**Difficulty Levels**: Beginner, Intermediate, Advanced
**Progression**: Linear progression with deload weeks

---

## Progressive Difficulty Phases

### PHASE 1: FOUNDATION (Weeks 1-4, Days 1-28)
**Focus**: Form, consistency, building base strength
**Intensity**: 60-70% of max effort
**Volume**: Moderate sets and reps

### PHASE 2: BUILDING (Weeks 5-8, Days 29-56)
**Focus**: Strength gains, muscle endurance
**Intensity**: 70-80% of max effort
**Volume**: Increased sets and reps

### PHASE 3: INTENSIFICATION (Weeks 9-12, Days 57-84)
**Focus**: Peak strength, power development
**Intensity**: 80-90% of max effort
**Volume**: High intensity, strategic volume

### PHASE 4: COMPLETION (Week 13, Days 85-90)
**Focus**: Testing, celebration, maintenance planning
**Intensity**: Peak testing followed by recovery
**Volume**: Test week followed by active recovery

---

**Focus**: Compound movements, explosive power

**Spiritual Connection**: Perseverance and finishing strong

**Focus**: Active recovery, spiritual renewal

## WEEK 1: DAYS 1-7 - Foundation Begins

### Day 1: Upper Body Push - Introduction
**Beginner**: 3 sets each
- Push-ups: 8-10 reps (knees if needed)
- Pike Push-ups: 6-8 reps
- Bench Dips: 8-10 reps
- Plank: 20-30 seconds

**Intermediate**: 4 sets each
- Push-ups: 12-15 reps
- Pike Push-ups: 10-12 reps
- Dips: 8-10 reps
- Plank: 45-60 seconds

**Advanced**: 5 sets each
- Push-ups: 20-25 reps
- Pike Push-ups: 15-18 reps
- Dips: 12-15 reps
- Plank: 60-90 seconds

**Spiritual Connection**: "Today you begin pushing against the resistance in your life. Every rep is a choice to be strong."

---

### Day 2: Lower Body Strength - Building Base
**Beginner**: 3 sets each
- Bodyweight Squats: 12-15 reps
- Lunges: 8-10 each leg
- Glute Bridges: 12-15 reps
- Calf Raises: 12-15 reps

**Intermediate**: 4 sets each
- Squats: 15-20 reps
- Lunges: 12-15 each leg
- Single-leg Deadlifts: 10-12 each leg
- Calf Raises: 15-20 reps

**Advanced**: 5 sets each
- Squats: 25-30 reps
- Bulgarian Split Squats: 12-15 each leg
- Single-leg Deadlifts: 15-18 each leg
- Single-leg Calf Raises: 15-20 each leg

**Spiritual Connection**: "Your legs are your foundation. Build them strong like the foundation of your faith - unshakeable."

---

### Day 3: Upper Body Pull - First Pull
**Beginner**: 3 sets each
- Inverted Rows: 6-8 reps
- Doorway Rows: 8-10 reps
- Wall Pull-aparts: 10-12 reps
- Dead Hangs: 10-20 seconds

**Intermediate**: 4 sets each
- Pull-ups (or assisted): 6-10 reps
- Inverted Rows: 10-12 reps
- Bicep Curls: 10-12 reps
- Face Pulls: 12-15 reps

**Advanced**: 5 sets each
- Pull-ups: 10-15 reps
- Wide-grip Pull-ups: 8-12 reps
- Bicep Curls: 12-15 reps
- Face Pulls: 15-18 reps

**Spiritual Connection**: "Pull yourself up. Just as you draw near to God, He draws near to you. Keep pulling."

---

### Day 4: Core & Conditioning - Foundation Core
**Beginner**: 3 rounds
- Crunches: 10-12 reps
- Bicycle Crunches: 8-10 each side
- Plank: 20-30 seconds
- Jumping Jacks: 20-30 reps

**Intermediate**: 4 rounds
- Sit-ups: 15-20 reps
- Russian Twists: 20-30 total
- Mountain Climbers: 30-45 seconds
- Burpees: 8-10 reps

**Advanced**: 5 rounds
- V-ups: 12-15 reps
- Russian Twists (weighted): 30-40 total
- Mountain Climbers: 45-60 seconds
- Burpees: 12-15 reps

**Spiritual Connection**: "Your core - your identity in Christ - must be solid. Build it daily."

---

### Day 5: Full Body Power - Introduction
**Beginner**: 3 rounds
- Bodyweight Squats: 10-12 reps
- Push-ups: 6-8 reps
- Lunges: 8-10 each leg
- Plank to Downward Dog: 6-8 reps

**Intermediate**: 4 rounds
- Jump Squats: 10-12 reps
- Push-up to T: 8-10 each side
- Step-ups: 12-15 each leg
- Plank to Pike: 10-12 reps

**Advanced**: 5 rounds
- Jump Squats: 15-18 reps
- Explosive Push-ups: 10-12 reps
- Box Jumps: 12-15 reps
- Burpee to Pike: 10-12 reps

**Spiritual Connection**: "God calls for your whole being - body, mind, spirit - all working together."

---

### Day 6: Challenge Day - Week 1 Test
**Beginner**: Complete
- 50 Push-ups (total, any rest needed)
- 50 Squats (total, any rest needed)
- 50 Sit-ups (total, any rest needed)
- 5-minute walk/jog

**Intermediate**: Complete
- 100 Push-ups (total, break as needed)
- 100 Squats (total, break as needed)
- 100 Sit-ups (total, break as needed)
- 10-minute run or jump rope

**Advanced**: Complete
- 150 Push-ups (total, break as needed)
- 150 Squats (total, break as needed)
- 150 Sit-ups (total, break as needed)
- 15-minute run or jump rope

**Spiritual Connection**: "Perseverance. You finish what you start. This is who you are."

---

### Day 7: SABBATH REST - Active Recovery

**NOT A "DO NOTHING" DAY**
This is intentional rest with purpose. Rest is commanded, not suggested.

**Morning Routine (20-30 minutes)**:
- Gentle full-body stretching (10-15 min)
- Deep breathing exercises (5 min)
- Gratitude journaling (5-10 min)

**Physical Activities (Choose 1-2)**:
- Light walk in nature (30-60 min)
- Gentle yoga or mobility work (20-30 min)
- Swimming or easy bike ride (30-45 min)
- Playing with kids (active but not intense)

**Spiritual Practices (Choose 2-3)**:
- Extended prayer time (20-30 min)
- Scripture meditation (15-20 min)
- Church/worship service
- Family devotions or discussion
- Sabbath meal with intentional conversation

**Rest Activities (Choose 1-2)**:
- Reading (physical book, not screen)
- Hobby time (woodworking, art, music)
- Quality time with wife (conversation, walk, date)
- Mentor/disciple someone (coffee, phone call)
- Nap (30-60 min, guilt-free)

**What to AVOID**:
- Intense workouts or training
- Work emails or projects
- Endless screen scrolling
- Mindless entertainment binging
- Isolation (rest with people you love)

**Spiritual Connection**: "God rested. Jesus rested. You must rest. It's not weakness - it's wisdom. You rest not because you're done, but to prepare for what's next."

## WEEK 2: DAYS 8-14 - Building Momentum

### Days 8-13: Same Pattern, Increased Volume
- Add 2-3 reps to each exercise
- Add 5-10 seconds to holds
- Decrease rest time by 5-10 seconds

### Day 14: SABBATH REST (Same structure as Day 7)

---

## WEEK 3: DAYS 15-21 - Consistency Builds

### Days 15-20: Progressive Overload
- Add 1 set to main exercises
- Increase time under tension
- Focus on form perfection

### Day 21: SABBATH REST (Same structure as Day 7)

---

## WEEK 4: DAYS 22-28 - DELOAD WEEK

### Days 22-27: Recovery and Preparation
**Purpose**: Allow body to recover, prevent burnout, prepare for Phase 2
**Volume**: 60-70% of Week 3
**Intensity**: Focus on form, slower tempo
**Mental**: This is NOT quitting - this is strategic rest

### Day 28: SABBATH REST (Same structure as Day 7)

---

## PHASE 2: WEEKS 5-8 (DAYS 29-56) - BUILDING

### Progressive Increases:
- **Week 5**: Add tempo training (3-1-3: 3 down, 1 pause, 3 up)
- **Week 6**: Add isometric holds at difficulty points
- **Week 7**: Increase volume by 10-15%
- **Week 8**: DELOAD (60-70% volume)

### Workout Pattern Continues:
- Day 1: Upper Push (add explosive push-ups)
- Day 2: Lower Body (add jump variations)
- Day 3: Upper Pull (add chin-ups variation)
- Day 4: Core & Conditioning (add circuit training)
- Day 5: Full Body (add complexes)
- Day 6: Challenge (increase volume by 25%)
- Day 7: SABBATH (extended stretching, deeper rest)

---

## PHASE 3: WEEKS 9-12 (DAYS 57-84) - INTENSIFICATION

### Progressive Increases:
- **Week 9**: Add advanced variations (one-arm push-ups, pistol squats)
- **Week 10**: Add weighted movements (backpack, dumbbells)
- **Week 11**: Peak week - highest volume and intensity
- **Week 12**: DELOAD (50-60% volume)

### Workout Intensity:
- Beginner: Mastering intermediate movements
- Intermediate: Adding advanced variations
- Advanced: Peak performance, testing limits

### Challenge Days:
- 200 reps total (push, pull, legs, core)
- Time-based challenges (max reps in 20 min)
- EMOM (Every Minute On the Minute) workouts

---

## PHASE 4: WEEK 13 (DAYS 85-90) - COMPLETION

### Day 85: TESTING DAY
**Purpose**: See how far you've come
**Structure**: Max rep testing on major movements
- Max push-ups in 2 minutes
- Max pull-ups unbroken
- Max squats in 2 minutes
- Max plank hold
- 1-mile run for time

**Spiritual Connection**: "This is your testimony. Look at what God has done through discipline and dedication."

---

### Day 86: Victory Lap
**Purpose**: Celebrate without stopping
**Structure**: Moderate full-body workout
- Every exercise from Week 1, but at current strength
- Show yourself where you started vs. where you are

**Spiritual Connection**: "Remember the journey. Give thanks for strength gained."

---

### Day 87: Warrior Session
**Purpose**: One final push
**Structure**: Your favorite challenging workout
- Choose the workout that pushed you most
- Complete it with excellence

**Spiritual Connection**: "Finish strong. You're not the same man who started 87 days ago."

---

### Day 88: Active Recovery
**Purpose**: Begin transition to maintenance
**Structure**: Light movement, long stretching
- Focus on mobility and flexibility
- Prepare body for what's next

**Spiritual Connection**: "Even after the battle, a warrior maintains his strength."

---

### Day 89: Family Strength Day
**Purpose**: Share your strength
**Structure**: Active time with family
- Teach your kids/wife some exercises
- Play active games together
- Pass on what you've learned

**Spiritual Connection**: "A strong man builds others up. Your transformation inspires transformation."

---

### Day 90: FINAL SABBATH
**Purpose**: Rest, reflect, plan forward
**Structure**: Extended Sabbath practices

**Morning**: 
- Extended prayer and thanksgiving (30-60 min)
- Journal the entire 90-day journey
- List every victory, every struggle, every lesson

**Afternoon**:
- Celebration meal with family
- Share your testimony with brothers
- Communion/worship

**Evening**:
- Plan for maintenance (what's next?)
- Commit to lifelong strength
- Set new goals

**Spiritual Connection**: "You built a wall in 90 days. Now maintain it for a lifetime. This is not the end - it's the beginning of who you've become."

## Exercise Progression Guide

### Push-ups Progression:
1. **Beginner**: Wall push-ups → Incline → Knee → Full
2. **Intermediate**: Full → Wide → Diamond → Decline
3. **Advanced**: One-arm progression → Explosive → Planche holds

### Pull-ups Progression:
1. **Beginner**: Dead hangs → Negatives → Assisted → Full
2. **Intermediate**: Full → Wide → Chin-ups → Mixed grip
3. **Advanced**: Weighted → One-arm progression → Muscle-ups

### Squats Progression:
1. **Beginner**: Box squats → Bodyweight → Goblet
2. **Intermediate**: Jump squats → Bulgarian splits → Pistol progression
3. **Advanced**: Weighted → Pistol squats → Shrimp squats

### Core Progression:
1. **Beginner**: Crunches → Sit-ups → Leg raises
2. **Intermediate**: V-ups → Dragon flags progression → L-sits
3. **Advanced**: Ab wheel → Front lever progression → Human flag

## Workout Principles

### Progressive Overload:
- Add reps/sets each week
- Decrease rest time
- Increase difficulty of movements
- Add tempo/isometric variations
- Add weight when bodyweight becomes easy

### Recovery:
- Sleep 7-9 hours nightly
- Hydrate (half bodyweight in ounces)
- Nutrition focused on protein and whole foods
- Stretch daily
- Honor Sabbath rest

### Injury Prevention:
- Always warm up (5-10 min)
- Focus on form over reps
- Listen to your body
- Don't train through sharp pain
- Deload when scheduled

### Mental Toughness:
- "Good" mindset on hard days
- No zero days
- Workout even when you don't feel like it
- The discipline is the point
- Your body can handle more than your mind thinks

## Sabbath Rest Guidelines (Every 7th Day)

### Purpose of Sabbath:
Not laziness - intentional recovery
Not doing nothing - doing different things
Not weakness - wisdom
Not stopping - strategic pausing

### Physical Rest:
- No intense workouts
- Active recovery (walking, stretching, swimming)
- Extended stretching routine (30-60 min)
- Foam rolling and mobility work
- Ice baths or sauna (if available)

### Spiritual Renewal:
- Extended prayer time (30+ min)
- Scripture reading and meditation
- Church/worship gathering
- Communion with God and family
- Sabbath meals (intentional, slow, grateful)

### Relational Investment:
- Quality time with wife (unrushed)
- Present time with children (phones away)
- Connect with brothers (encourage, check in)
- Serve others (visit sick, help neighbor)
- Family activities (hike, games, conversation)

### Mental Rest:
- No work emails or projects
- Limit screens and social media
- Read physical books
- Journal and reflect
- Nap without guilt (30-60 min)

### Hobby Time:
- Woodworking, building, creating
- Playing music or art
- Cooking/grilling
- Gardening or yard work (peaceful, not rushed)
- Any hobby that brings life without stress

### What NOT to Do:
- Intense workouts or training
- Work on projects or business
- Endless TV/screen binging
- Isolating yourself
- Feeling guilty for resting

### The Sabbath Mindset:
"I rest not because my work is done, but because God is good. I rest not in weakness, but in faith. I rest to be restored, renewed, and ready for battle. This is obedience, not laziness."

## Difficulty Level Adjustments

### BEGINNER (0-6 months training):
- Focus on form and consistency
- Build base strength and endurance
- Start with easier variations
- Longer rest periods (60-90 seconds)
- Goal: Complete all 90 days with excellent form

### INTERMEDIATE (6-24 months training):
- Increase volume and intensity
- Add complex movements
- Shorter rest periods (45-60 seconds)
- Begin adding weight or difficulty
- Goal: Master advanced variations

### ADVANCED (24+ months training):
- Peak intensity and volume
- Advanced variations only
- Minimal rest (30-45 seconds)
- Weighted movements standard
- Goal: Push absolute limits while maintaining form
